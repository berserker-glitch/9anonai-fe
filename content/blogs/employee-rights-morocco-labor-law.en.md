## The Foundation of Labor Law Advice Morocco: Law 65.99

The Moroccan Labor Code is the cornerstone of all professional relationships in the private sector. It defines the rules for recruitment, the execution of work, and the termination of contracts. When seeking **labor law advice Morocco**, it is important to distinguish between different types of employment contracts:

1.  **Fixed-Term Contracts (CDD):** Used for specific tasks and limited durations.
2.  **Indefinite-Term Contracts (CDI):** The standard form of employment providing greater long-term security.
3.  **Temporary Work:** Regulated strictly to prevent the exploitation of casual labor.

According to Law 65.99, every employee is entitled to a written contract (especially for foreign workers) and a monthly payslip. These documents serve as the primary evidence of your **حقوق الموظف في المغرب** should a dispute arise.

## Specialized Protections for Different Sectors

Moroccan law recognizes that different professions require specific regulatory nuances. Recent legislative updates have expanded the scope of **employee rights Morocco** to include previously underserved groups:

### Social Workers and Professional Ethics
Under Law No. 45.18, the profession of social workers is now strictly regulated. Article 8 of this law stipulates that practicing as a social worker requires prior authorization from the competent government authority. However, this specialized regulation does not bypass the general protections of the Labor Code; it works in tandem with Law 65.99 to ensure these professionals enjoy both professional recognition and standard **droits des employés Maroc**.

### Domestic Workers
A significant milestone in Moroccan labor law was the implementation of Law No. 19.12. This law brought domestic workers into the legal fold, granting them rights to minimum wage, weekly rest, and paid annual leave—rights that were previously informal.

### Public Sector and Academy Employees
For those working in state institutions, such as the Academy of the Kingdom of Morocco (regulated by Law 74.19), the law ensures a "continuity of rights." Article 55 of this law guarantees that when institutions are reorganized, employees retain all their acquired rights, including their pension schemes and seniority.

## Social Security and Healthcare: Essential Rights

A major component of **droit du travail Maroc conseil** involves social protection. It is not enough to simply receive a salary; an employer is legally obligated to register employees with the National Social Security Fund (CNSS).

### Mandatory Health Insurance (AMO)
Morocco has made significant strides in universal health coverage. The law mandates that employees and retirees from both the public and private sectors must be affiliated with mutual associations or the AMO system. For government and local authority employees, Law No. 31.99 makes it mandatory to join mutual societies, with contributions often deducted at the source to ensure continuous coverage.

### Pension Schemes
Whether you are under the Moroccan Pension Fund (CMR) for public servants or the CNSS for the private sector, your right to a retirement pension is protected by law. Even during institutional transitions (as seen in Reference 1 regarding the Academy of the Kingdom), the law explicitly states that employees remain affiliated with their original pension funds to protect their future financial stability.

## Employment Rights for Foreign Workers in Morocco

Morocco is an attractive destination for international talent. However, foreign employees must adhere to specific procedures to ensure their **employee rights Morocco** are enforceable. 

Ministerial Decree No. 19.1430 sets a specific model for the employment contracts of foreigners. Under Article 517 of the Labor Code, a foreign worker’s contract must be approved by the Ministry of Labor. This "visa" on the contract is what legalizes the employment. Without this formalization, a foreign worker may find it difficult to claim certain legal protections or resolve disputes in Moroccan labor courts.

## The Role of the Ministry of Labor and Inspection

The Ministry of Labor and Professional Integration is the executive body responsible for enforcing **حقوق الموظف في المغرب**. The ministry is structured into several specialized departments:
*   **Labor Inspection Service:** Inspectors have the authority to visit workplaces, ensure safety standards are met, and mediate between employers and employees.
*   **Occupational Medicine:** Focuses on the health and safety of workers within the workplace.
*   **International Relations:** Manages the rights of Moroccan workers abroad and foreign workers within Morocco.
