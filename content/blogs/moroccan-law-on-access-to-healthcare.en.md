# Moroccan Law on Access to Healthcare

The right to health is a fundamental pillar of any modern society, and in the Kingdom of Morocco, this right has undergone significant legislative evolution over the past decade. The Moroccan legal framework aims to create a balanced ecosystem where medical expertise, infrastructure, and financial protection converge to serve the citizen.

Understanding the laws governing access to healthcare is essential for both residents and professionals. From the rights of patients to the regulations governing medical practitioners, Moroccan law provides a structured path toward universal health coverage and high-quality medical services.

## The Constitutional and Legislative Foundation of Health Rights

The Moroccan legal system enshrines the right to health as a core principle. This is primarily articulated through **Law No. 65.00**, known as the **Code of Basic Health Coverage (Code de la Couverture Médicale de Base)**. As recently amended by Law No. 27.22 in November 2022, this legislation marks a historic shift toward "Mandatory Basic Health Insurance" (Assurance Maladie Obligatoire - AMO).

The preamble of Law No. 65.00 emphasizes that the state is committed to collective and solidarity-based management of health expenses. The primary goal is to ensure that all social segments, regardless of their income, can access medical services. This transition from a fragmented system to a unified, mandatory insurance model is designed to ensure the financial sustainability of the healthcare sector while protecting the dignity of the patient.

## Professional Standards and the Role of Specialists

Access to healthcare is not merely about insurance; it is about the quality and availability of qualified medical professionals. Moroccan law strictly regulates who can practice medicine and under what titles.

Under **Royal Decree (Decret Royal) No. 46.66**, the titles of "Specialist" and "Professor" (Mubarriz) are legally protected. This ensures that when a patient seeks specialized care, the practitioner has met rigorous national standards. Furthermore, the healthcare system is organized around a "Care Pathway" (Maslak al-Ilyajat). According to **Article 13 of the Framework Law on the National Health System**, patients are encouraged to start their medical journey at primary healthcare facilities in the public sector or with a general practitioner in the private sector. This structured approach ensures that specialized resources are used efficiently and that patients receive the most appropriate level of care.

## International Expertise and Foreign Practitioners

To enhance the quality of the national health offer, Morocco has opened its doors to international medical expertise. **Law No. 131.13**, relating to the practice of medicine, and its subsequent implementing decrees, provide a framework for non-resident doctors to practice in the Kingdom.

According to **Article 9** of the implementing decree for Law No. 131.13, a director of a public or private health institution can request authorization for a foreign, non-resident doctor to perform temporary medical interventions. The requirements are strict to protect patient safety:
*   The doctor must provide a certified diploma equivalent to a specialist degree.
*   Proof of registration with the medical board in their home country is mandatory.
*   The practitioner must hold a civil liability insurance contract covering medical acts performed within Morocco.

This openness, as highlighted in **Article 14 of the Framework Law**, aims to attract Moroccan talents living abroad and foreign experts to share knowledge and improve the overall quality of services provided to Moroccan citizens.

## Financial Protection and Insurance Obligations

A critical component of accessing healthcare is the insurance infrastructure. The **Insurance Code (Law No. 17.99)** and subsequent amendments like **Law No. 64.12** govern how risks are covered within the territory. 

**Article 162 of the Insurance Code** stipulates that risks located in Morocco, including health-related liabilities, must generally be insured by companies authorized and resident in Morocco. This ensures that the Moroccan Supervisory Authority of Insurance and Social Welfare (ACAPS) can oversee the protection of policyholders. 

For Moroccans residing abroad (MRE) who choose to transfer their fiscal residence back to Morocco, specialized laws such as **Law No. 63.14** provide a framework for declaring foreign assets. This is relevant to healthcare access as it stabilizes the financial and legal status of returning citizens, allowing them to integrate seamlessly into the national social protection and health insurance systems.

## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

The Moroccan legal landscape regarding healthcare is moving toward a more inclusive, regulated, and high-quality system. By combining mandatory insurance with strict professional standards and an openness to global medical expertise, the law seeks to guarantee the right to health for all.

**Key Takeaways:**
*   **Universal Coverage:** Law No. 65.00 establishes mandatory health insurance as a right for all citizens based on social solidarity.
*   **Structured Care:** Patients should follow the defined "Care Pathway," starting with primary care or general practitioners.
*   **Regulated Quality:** Only practitioners meeting the criteria of Royal Decree 46.66 and Law 131.13 can claim specialist titles or practice medicine.
*   **Global Integration:** Recent laws facilitate the contribution of international medical experts to the Moroccan health system while maintaining strict safety and insurance standards.
